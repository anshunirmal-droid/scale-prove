Learn from others' mistakes

Six things this cohort almost always gets wrong

Patterns our founders have observed across hundreds of launches and scale-ups. The meta-mistake underneath all of them: treating "going global" as a distribution decision, not a business-building one.

01
Product-first, not market-first

Assuming India's winning SKU is automatically the US's winning SKU.

02
Underestimating landed cost

Freight, duty, FBA fees, returns and ad spend quietly eat the margin.

03
Ads as a Day-1 lever

Ads amplify a listing that already converts — they don't fix one that doesn't.

04
No inventory forecast

Out of stock at peak kills ranking; over-ordering triggers storage fees.

05
Compliance as an afterthought

IEC, LUT and ICEGATE delays hold shipments for weeks when left too late.

06
No plan B

No liquidation path, no pivot playbook, no lever if the product stalls.
